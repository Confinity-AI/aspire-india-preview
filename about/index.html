About

A people's movement for every child

Twelve years, one idea, one and a half million children.
Who we are

Most children in India go to a government school. We work to make those schools good enough to stay in.

Every child in school, learning well, and finishing — in a government school system that the community itself runs and protects.
How we work

Three parts, and they only work together

01 · Access

Get every child in the door

Door-to-door surveys find the children who never enrolled or quietly dropped out. Bridge courses catch them up and walk them back into class.

02 · Learning

Make the hours count

Teachers get training and materials. Children who have fallen behind get help until they haven't — and learn to keep learning on their own.

03 · Governance

Put the village in charge

School committees, parent groups and panchayats learn to run — and demand — a good school. Then, in the end, they don't need us.

Our journey

From ten Delhi schools to five states

The same model, grown patiently over twelve years.
2014
Ten schools in Delhi
ASPIRE begins with learning support and teacher training for 2,204 children across ten South Delhi municipal schools, funded by Tata AIG.
2014
The Thousand Schools Project
With Tata Steel, we take on 900+ government schools across six blocks of Odisha — the start of what's now our biggest programme.
2015
Girls first
Work begins in a government-aided girls' school with Tech Mahindra Foundation, walking first-generation learners from Class 6 towards Class 12.
2017
Jharkhand
Bridge courses open in Paschim Singhbhum with The Hans Foundation — residential and non-residential routes back into school.
2019
Child Labour Free Zones
Villages begin declaring themselves free of child labour — and holding the line. 6,561 habitations carry the declaration today.
2022
Chhattisgarh: Jan Pahal
A whole-block programme opens in Korba with RIST and The Hans Foundation — 57,000 children across 574 schools.
2026
Today
1.5 million children across five states — and a model whole districts can run for themselves.
What we believe

Four convictions we don't compromise on

01

Communities can lead

Communities have the will and the power to change things. We help them do it.

02

Education is a public good

It is the state's job to provide it. We work to make government schools stronger.

03

Teachers come first

Children cannot thrive if teachers are held back. We invest in teachers.

04

Every child can grow

Given the chance, every child can reach their potential. We create that chance.
